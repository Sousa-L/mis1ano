ONTEM CHOVEU NO FUTURO¹

Do you know the Cearás?
Just like that, in the plural?

This immersive audiovisual installation poses a series of questions to which we have very few answers.

In this work, the most pleasurable challenge is to take risks.

The fundamental condition of taking risks is to make mistakes. To err and discover, to err again and innovate, to err and subvert, in short, to err and learn.

This Museum is infinite, as well as the many Cearás.

The installation embraces us and takes us to the epicenter of these expanded screens, featuring powerful projectors aimed at all possible surfaces and ambisonic sound.

It's a dive. Nothing is certain and conclusive in this work. Nor is it intended to be definitive, so don't be surprised if you lose ground.

The immersive audiovisual experience is one of the possibilities to free ourselves from reality.

You, spectator-actor, are a flyer who will act alongside the story that is being told, always being inside it. So let yourself fly. Fly. Don't limit yourself to seeing and hearing. Use a poetic jargon from the Pantanal that says: “If you want to see, listen”. Shuffle your senses and try to LISEETEN.

In a restless, intuitive, undisciplined way, but always grounded, here are several artistic fragments collectively sewn in handmade-digital confection by us and by the chosen artists from Ceará with their respective works.

Enchanted by the charm during the trips to the many Cearás that I have known, I heard one of the most exuberant images: Tá Bonito Pra Chover.²

It has urgency, vitality, imagination and poetry. It is possible to close your eyes and listen to the rain in the future.

This inaugural artistic project that houses the installation ONTEM CHOVEU NO FUTURO has a complementary training project to the master class EU ME INTERESSO PELAS COISAS QUE NUNCA FIZ³, held at the inauguration of this space, as well as an immersive atelier MESTRE É QUEM DE REPENTE APRENDE4 where we share our entire technical-creative process with local artists.

The educator and revolutionary Paulo Freire, with his pedagogy of autonomy (and curiosity), stated: “Let us learn by teaching ourselves.”

The process of creation and construction was and will always be done by a collective of knowledge of people who complement each other with their powers and limitations for a loving delivery.

This work is intentionally unfinished because it has no beginning and will never end. Enter whenever you want and never leave. This work is a journey of great availability, and also ruptures, affections, bridges to better see another world. A world that does not yet exist, but which we will never give up building.

It has urgency, vitality, imagination and poetry. It is possible to close your eyes and listen to the rain in the future.

Batman Zavareze
Author and General Director of the work ONTEM CHOVEU NO FUTURO.

TRANSLATOR’S NOTES

1 YESTERDAY IT RAINED IN THE FUTURE in free translation. It was taken from the poem “Dia um”, inserted in the book O livro das ignorãnças, by the Brazilian writer Manoel de Barros.

2 “It’s a Beautiful Sky For The Rain To Come” in free translation. In the northeast of Brazil, the expression "It's beautiful for something to happen" is often used to refer to the weather when the rain is coming.

3 “I am interested in things I have never done” in free translation.

4 “Master is who suddenly learns”; phrase by the Brazilian writer Guimarães Rosa.
